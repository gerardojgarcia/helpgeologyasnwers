Minerals

Everything you wanted to know about minerals.

Chapter Summary

Minerals are naturally occurring, solid substances. Formed by geologic processes. With a definable chemical composition and an internal structure characterized by an orderly arrangement of atoms, ions or molecules in a crystalline lattice. Most minerals are inorganic.

Biogenic minerals are produced by organisms. The minerals in shells are an example of biomineralization.

in the crystalline lattice of minerals, atoms occur in a specific pattern-one of nature’s first expels of ordering.

minerals can form by solidification of a melt, precipitation from a water solution, diffusion through a solid, the metabolism of organisms, and precipitation from a gas.

About 4,000 different types of minerals are known, each with a name and distinctive physical properties ( cleavage, magnetism and reactivity with acid. ) the unique physical properties of a mineral reflect its chemical composition and crystal structure . By observing these physical properties, you can identify minerals.

the most convenient way for classifying minerals is to group them according to their carbonates, and native metals.

silicate minerals are the most common minerals on Earth, the silicon-oxygen building block of silicate minerals.

groups of silicate minerals are distinguished from each another by the ways in which the silicon-oxygen tetrahedra that constitute them are linked. gemstones are minerals known for their beauty and rarity. The facets on cut gems used in jewelry are made by grind nada polishing the stones with a faceting machine.

Key Topics
  • minerals are identified as using physics properties. The most commonly used properties are streak, color, hardness, luster, specifics gravity, crystal habit, and cleavage.
  • minerals making up rock are formed in five ways: crystallization inform a melt,. Solid state diffusion through a preexisting crystal. Precipitation form a water solution, precipitation from a water solution. Precipitation forma gas, and biomineralization ( the activities of organisms)
  • minerals are classified based on the anions or anionic groups that they contain. Minerals lacking anions are termed native elements. Other important groups are oxides, sulfides, halides, sulfates, and carbonates.
  • vast majority of earth’s minerals are silicates.
  • the degree of oxygen sharing among the tetrahedra that for m the basis of these minerals allows for the great diversity of crystalline forms in silicates.
